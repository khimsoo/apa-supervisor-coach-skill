## 1. Routine Check-in (30 min)

Purpose: maintain alignment, address immediate needs, course-correct early. Frequency: monthly or quarterly.

**Opening — Warm & Genuine (5 min)**
> "How are you doing? How's the week been treating you?"

*[Listen genuinely. This sets the tone for psychological safety.]*

**Acknowledge Recent Wins (2 min)**
> "I noticed [specific recent accomplishment]. That was great work because [specific impact]."

**Impact Update (10 min)** — Ask about current priorities:
- "What are you working on right now?"
- "Walk me through where you are on [specific project]."
- "What's the current status of [key deliverable]?"
- "What progress have you made since we last talked?"
- "What's the impact of this work? Who's it helping?"

*Listen for: Progress, blockers, clarity on priorities, engagement level.*

**Challenge Navigation (5 min)** — Ask about obstacles:
- "What's getting in your way?"
- "Are there any blockers I should know about?"
- "What would help you move faster?"
- "Do you have what you need to succeed?"
- "Is there anything I can unblock for you?"

*Listen for: Resource gaps, unclear expectations, interpersonal issues, skill gaps.*

**Strategic Clarification (5 min)** — Confirm alignment:
- "How does this connect to [team/division goal]?"
- "Do you understand why this matters?"
- "Is this still the right priority?"
- "Are we aligned on what success looks like?"

**Forward Planning (3 min)** — Confirm next steps:
- "What's your focus for the next [week/month]?"
- "What do you need from me?"
- "When should we check in next?"
- "Is there anything else on your mind?"

**Close**
> "Great. I appreciate the update. Keep me posted on [specific item]."

## 2. Mid-Year Checkpoint (60 min)

Purpose: evaluate progress, recalibrate goals, provide feedback, plan development.

**Opening — Reflective (5 min)**
> "We're halfway through the year. I want to step back and reflect on how things are going. How are you feeling about your progress?"

**Goal Progress Review (15 min)** — review each goal:
- "Let's walk through [Goal 1]. Where are you on this?"
- "What's been going well? What's been challenging?"
- "What's the current status — on track, ahead, or behind?"
- "What do you need to get back on track if needed?"
- "What's the impact of this work so far?"

For each goal clarify: progress toward completion, quality of work, impact delivered, any changes needed.

**Schema & Behaviours Assessment (10 min)** — how they're working:
- "How are you showing up as a [role title]?"
- "Tell me about a time you [specific behaviour — collaborated, solved a problem, took initiative]."
- "How are you doing on [specific schema element]?"
- "What's one area where you feel you're really strong?"
- "What's one area where you want to grow?"

**Feedback Integration (10 min)** — share feedback themes:
- "I've been gathering feedback from your collaborators. Here are some themes I'm hearing..."
- "People appreciate [specific strength]."
- "One area for development is [specific area]."
- "How does this land with you? What's your perspective?"

**Development Planning (15 min)**:
- "Based on your goals and feedback, what do you want to focus on for development?"
- "What's one skill you want to strengthen?"
- "How can I support your development?"
- "What resources or opportunities would help?"

**Next Steps & Alignment (5 min)**:
- "For the second half of the year, here's what I want to focus on..."
- "Do you have any adjustments to your goals?"
- "What support do you need from me?"

**Close**
> "I appreciate your work so far this year. I'm excited about [specific area]. Let's make the second half even stronger."

## 3. Year-End Review (90 min)

Purpose: complete assessment, discuss rating, plan next year, discuss career.

**Opening — Appreciative (5 min)**
> "We're at the end of the year. I want to take time to reflect on your contributions and discuss your assessment and next year. Thank you for your work this year."

**Impact Showcase (20 min)** — walk through achievements:
- "Let's start with [major project/achievement]. Tell me about your role and impact."
- "What was the challenge? How did you approach it?"
- "What was the outcome? What was the impact?"
- "What did you learn from this?"

Repeat for 2–3 major achievements. For each: understand their perspective on impact, clarify their specific contribution, assess against impact/independence/consistency, note examples for calibration.

**Goal Achievement Review (10 min)**:
- "Let's review your goals for the year. [Goal 1]: What's the final status?"
- "What did you deliver? What's the impact?"
- "What got in the way, if anything?"
- "What would you do differently?"

**Stakeholder Impact & Feedback (10 min)**:
- "I gathered feedback from your collaborators and stakeholders. Here are the themes:"
- "People highlight [specific strength] as a real asset."
- "One area where you've grown is [specific area]."
- "An area for continued development is [specific area]."
- "How does this feedback land? What's your perspective?"

**Development Review (10 min)**:
- "We identified [development priority] at mid-year. How did you work on this?"
- "What progress have you made?"
- "What's still important to develop?"

**Rating Discussion (15 min)**:
- "Based on everything we've discussed — your impact, your independence, your consistency — here's how I'm assessing your performance: [Rating]."
- "Here's why: [Specific examples and reasoning]"
- "How does this feel to you? Do you see it differently?"

If they disagree:
- "Help me understand your perspective."
- "What am I missing?"
- "Here's what I'm seeing: [specific examples]. How do you see it?"

**Career & Growth Conversation (15 min)**:
- "Looking ahead, where do you want to focus your energy?"
- "What's your career aspiration? Where do you see yourself in 2–3 years?"
- "What skills or experiences do you need to get there?"
- "I see potential for you in [specific direction]. What do you think?"

**Next Year Planning (5 min)**:
- "For next year, here are the priorities I see for your role..."
- "What are your goals? What do you want to focus on?"
- "What support do you need from me?"

**Close**
> "Thank you for your contributions this year. I'm excited about [specific area] and your growth. Let's make next year even stronger."

## 4. High Performer Retention

Used for proactive retention and growth conversations with your strongest officers.

**Opening — Recognition**
> "I wanted to sit down and talk about your impact and your future. You've been doing exceptional work, and I want to make sure you're challenged and growing."

**Acknowledge Impact**
> "Your work on [specific project/area] has made a real difference. Here's what I've observed: [specific examples, metrics, impact]."

**Explore Satisfaction**
> "How are you feeling about your work? Are you challenged? Are you learning?"

*[Listen for signs of boredom, frustration, or desire for growth.]*

**Understand Career Aspirations**
> "Where do you see your career heading? What would success look like for you in 2–3 years?"

**Offer Growth Opportunities**
> "Based on what you've shared, I'm thinking about [specific stretch assignment/opportunity] for you. This would [specific benefit]. What do you think?"

**Discuss Development**
> "What skills do you want to develop? What would accelerate your growth?"

**Expand Scope**
> "I'd like to expand your responsibilities to include [specific area]. This would give you visibility to [senior leaders/broader impact]."

**Discuss Career Path**
> "I see real potential for you to move into [specific direction]. Here's what I think you need to do to get there: [specific development plan]."
>
> "Here's what the next 12 months could look like for you: [specific milestones, projects, or promotions]."
>
> "What would need to be true for you to feel you're on the right track?"

**Explore Rotation Opportunities**
> "One of the most powerful ways to accelerate your growth is through a rotation — spending time in a different team or function."
>
> "Are there any areas of the organisation you're curious about or want to gain exposure to?"
>
> "I'm thinking [specific team/function] could be a strong fit for you because [reason]."
>
> "A rotation could be structured as [duration, e.g. 3–6 months] and would help you [specific benefit]."
>
> "High performers who've done rotations often come back with stronger networks, broader thinking, and clearer career direction. I'd like to invest in that for you."
>
> "Would you be open to exploring this? I can start conversations with [relevant manager/team] if you're interested."

**Close with Commitment**
> "I'm invested in your success and growth. Let's make sure you're challenged, learning, and moving toward your goals. I want to keep you engaged and growing here."

## 5. Retention and Career Growth (Proactive)

Similar to High Performer Retention but pitched for any officer you want to keep engaged, not only the top performer.

**Opening — Proactive**
> "I want to talk about your future here and make sure we're aligned on your career growth."

**Acknowledge Contributions**
> "You've been a valuable member of the team. Your work on [specific areas] has made a real difference."

**Ask About Satisfaction**
> "How are you feeling about your role? Are you challenged? Are you learning?"

**Explore Aspirations**
> "Where do you see yourself in 2–3 years? What's your ideal next role or responsibility?"

**Discuss Development Needs**
> "To get to where you want to go, what do you need to develop? What skills or experiences are important?"

**Offer Specific Opportunities**
> "Based on our conversation, I'm thinking about [specific opportunity]. This would help you develop [specific skill] and move toward [career goal]."

**Discuss Career Path**
> "Let's talk about what a clear path forward looks like for you here. Based on your aspirations, I see a few possible directions:"
>
> - "The [Role A] path — focused on deepening expertise in [area]. This typically involves [key milestones]."
> - "The [Role B] path — moving toward [leadership/broader scope]. This would require building [specific skills]."
>
> "Which of these resonates most with you? Are there other directions you're considering?"
>
> "Here's what I think the next 12 months could look like for you: [specific milestones, projects, or promotions]."
>
> "What would need to be true for you to feel confident you're on the right path?"

**Explore Rotation Opportunities**
> "One way to accelerate your growth is through a rotation — spending time in a different team or function to broaden your experience."
>
> "Are there any teams or areas of the organisation you're curious about or want to learn from?"
>
> "I'm thinking [specific team/function] could be a strong fit for you because [reason]."
>
> "A rotation could be structured as [duration, e.g. 3–6 months] and would help you [specific benefit]."
>
> "Would you be open to exploring this? I can start conversations with [relevant manager/team] if you're interested."

**Create Action Plan**
> "Here's what I'm committing to: [specific support, opportunities, timeline]. Here's what I need from you: [specific commitment]."

**Close with Commitment**
> "I'm invested in your success and want to see you thrive here. Let's make sure you're growing and moving toward your goals."

## 6. Underperformer Turnaround

Use when there is concrete evidence of underperformance and you want to create a structured path back.

**Opening — Supportive, Not Punitive**
> "I want to talk about how things are going. I've noticed some challenges, and I want to understand what's happening and how I can help."

**Describe Observations**
> "Here's what I'm seeing: [specific examples — missed deadlines, quality issues, collaboration concerns]. I want to understand what's behind this."

**Invite Their Perspective**
> "What's going on from your side? Are there obstacles I'm not seeing? Is there something I'm missing?"

*[Listen for root causes: skill gaps, personal issues, unclear expectations, wrong role fit, etc.]*

**Understand Root Cause**
> "So it sounds like [summarise]. Is that right? Help me understand: Is this a skill issue, a capacity issue, a clarity issue, or something else?"

**Express Confidence**
> "I believe you can turn this around. I've seen you do good work, and I want to help you get back there."

**Create Support Plan**
> "Here's what I'm going to do to support you: [specific support — more frequent check-ins, clearer expectations, resources, mentoring]."

**Set Clear Expectations**
> "By [date], I need to see [specific, measurable improvements]. We'll check in weekly to track progress."

**Offer Resources**
> "What do you need from me? What resources or support would help you succeed?"

**Close with Accountability**
> "I'm committed to helping you succeed. And I also need to see improvement. Let's work together on this."

Principle to hold: *Diagnose before prescribing — understand the root cause.* Document your support efforts and the officer's response. Know when to escalate: sometimes role fit is the issue.

## 7. Motivating Disengaged Underperformer

Use when the signal is disengagement, not skill gap — the officer is capable but has checked out.

**Opening — Empathetic**
> "I've noticed you seem less engaged lately. I want to check in and understand what's going on."

**Ask Open Questions**
> "How are you feeling about your work? What's been challenging? What's been rewarding?"

*[Listen for motivation issues: unclear purpose, lack of growth, feeling undervalued, wrong fit, etc.]*

**Understand the Root Cause**
> "It sounds like [summarise]. Help me understand: Is it the work itself, the team, the direction, or something else?"

**Reconnect to Purpose**
> "Let me remind you why this work matters. [Specific impact of their work]. Your contribution to [specific area] is important."

**Identify Strengths**
> "You're really good at [specific strength]. I want to make sure you're using those strengths in your work."

**Offer Change**
> "What if we [specific change — different project, new responsibility, different team dynamic]? Would that help?"

**Create Development Plan**
> "Let's work on [specific development area] together. This will help you grow and also make your work more rewarding."

**Expand Opportunities**
> "I want to give you more visibility and opportunity. How about [specific opportunity]?"

**Close with Engagement**
> "I believe in you and want to see you succeed. Let's work together to make your work more engaging and rewarding."

## 8. Difficult Conversation (general scaffold)

The generic shape for any high-stakes one-to-one — use when none of the specific scripts above fits exactly.

**Opening — Empathetic**
> "I want to talk about [specific situation]. I value our working relationship and want to address this directly."

**Describe the Situation — Specific**
> "Here's what I've observed: [specific examples, not generalisations]"

**Impact — Clear**
> "This has affected [specific impact on team/work/results]"

**Invite Their Perspective — Curious**
> "Help me understand your perspective. What's been going on from your side?"

*[Listen actively — do not interrupt.]*

**Clarify — Collaborative**
> "So what I'm hearing is... Is that right?"

**Move Forward — Solution-focused**
> "Here's what needs to change: [specific, measurable expectations]"

**Offer Support — Genuine**
> "Here's how I can support you: [specific support]"

**Confirm Understanding — Clear**
> "Let's make sure we're on the same page. What will you do differently?"

**Follow Up — Accountable**
> "Let's check in on [date] to see how things are going."

Key moves: prepare thoroughly — know what you want to say and why. Lead with empathy, not judgment. Use specific examples, not generalisations. Focus on behaviour and impact, not personality. Acknowledge their perspective before sharing yours.

## 9. Rating Disagreement

Use when an officer disagrees with their rating, either during year-end or post-results.

**Opening — Respectful**
> "I appreciate your perspective on this rating. I want to understand your thinking and share mine so we can reach alignment."

**Listen Fully**
> "Help me understand your rationale. What evidence are you seeing that supports this rating?"

*[Listen without interrupting. Take notes.]*

**Acknowledge**
> "I hear you. You're pointing to [specific examples]. I can see why that would suggest [their rating]."

**Share Your Perspective**
> "Here's what I'm seeing: [specific examples, impact, context]. This leads me to believe [your rating] is more accurate because [specific reasoning]."

**Explore Gaps**
> "Where do you think we're seeing things differently? Is it about the impact, the independence, or the consistency?"

**Seek Common Ground**
> "What if we look at it this way: [alternative framing]. Does that help bridge our perspectives?"

**Offer a Path Forward**
> "Let's gather more data. Can we [specific action] to help us both feel confident in the final rating?"

**Close**
> "I value your input. Let's make sure we're calibrated before we finalise this rating."

Key moves: listen fully — understand their perspective without interrupting. Acknowledge their feelings — validate emotion without changing the rating. Explain your rationale using specific examples. Offer a path forward — help them understand development priorities. Explain the calibration process and how ratings are determined relative to peers. Use specific evidence to support your assessment.

## 10. Bias in Assessment (self-check with a peer)

Use when you want to pressure-test your own assessments for bias — typically with a peer manager or your own manager, not with your officer.

**Opening — Curious, Not Defensive**
> "I want to talk about something important. I've been reflecting on my assessments and want to make sure I'm being fair and objective."

**Acknowledge the Concern**
> "I recognise that bias can creep in unintentionally. I want to check my thinking with you."

**Share Your Reflection**
> "Looking at [specific officer/group], I want to make sure I'm evaluating them on impact, not on [potential bias: similarity to me, communication style, background, etc.]."

**Ask for Perspective**
> "Do you see any patterns in my assessments that concern you? Are there officers I might be over- or under-rating?"

*[Listen openly. Don't get defensive.]*

**Review Evidence**
> "Let me walk through the evidence for [officer]. Here's what I'm seeing: [specific examples, metrics, feedback]. Does this feel fair to you?"

**Correct If Needed**
> "If I've missed something or been unfair, I want to adjust. What would a fair assessment look like?"

**Commit to Improvement**
> "Going forward, I'm going to [specific action] to ensure I'm being objective. I appreciate you helping me catch blind spots."

**Follow Up**
> "Let's check in again after calibration to make sure we're aligned on fairness."

## 11. Performance Conversation Prep Template

Use before any structured conversation (routine / mid-year / year-end) to prep yourself — not to share with the officer.

```
ROUTINE CHECK-IN (30 minutes)
Purpose: Maintain alignment and address immediate needs

Opening: "How are you doing? How's the week been treating you?"

Topics to cover:
• Recent wins and acknowledgements
• Current priorities and progress
• Blockers and support needed
• Next steps and follow-ups

MID-YEAR CHECKPOINT (60 minutes)
Purpose: Evaluate progress and recalibrate goals

Opening: "We're halfway through the year. I want to step back and reflect on how things are going."

Topics to cover:
• Goal progress review (each goal)
• Schema and behaviours assessment
• Feedback integration
• Development planning
• Next steps and alignment

YEAR-END REVIEW (90 minutes)
Purpose: Complete assessment and discuss rating

Opening: "We're at the end of the year. I want to take time to reflect on your contributions."

Topics to cover:
• Impact showcase (2–3 major achievements)
• Goal achievement review
• Stakeholder impact and feedback
• Development review
• Rating discussion
• Career and growth conversation
• Next year planning
```

## 13. Calibration Question Framework (prompts)

```
IMPACT QUESTIONS:
• What was the value delivered, and how long did it take?
• What has not landed yet but sets up future success?
• What indirect impact did they have?

INDEPENDENCE QUESTIONS:
• How much of the value was their responsibility?
• How much support and direction did they need?
• How did they manage and leverage other officers?

CONSISTENCY QUESTIONS:
• How often have they delivered at this level?
• Has performance been steady over time?
• Are there peaks and dips?

SUSTAINABILITY QUESTIONS:
• Can they maintain this performance without burnout?
• Do they have healthy work–life balance?
• Are they building sustainable practices?
```

## 14. Feedback Coaching Guide for Officers

Use when coaching *your officer* on how they should collect and interpret peer feedback for their self-evaluation.

```

Who has worked with them on significant projects?
[ ] Team members
[ ] Cross-functional partners
[ ] Stakeholders
[ ] Peers at similar level
[ ] People at different seniority levels

STEP 2: FRAME THE REQUEST
Example: "I'd like your perspective on my stakeholder management during the Q3
project from September to November. Specifically, how effectively did I keep
you informed about progress and changes?"

STEP 3: INTERPRET FEEDBACK
[ ] Look for patterns across multiple sources
[ ] Identify actionable insights
[ ] Separate feedback from judgment
[ ] Connect to development priorities

STEP 4: CREATE ACTION PLAN
Based on feedback, what will you do differently?
___________________________________________________________
___________________________________________________________
```

Short conversation starters an officer can use to request feedback:

- "I'd like your perspective on my stakeholder management during the Q3 customer portal project from September to November. Specifically, how effectively did I keep you informed about progress and changes?"
- "I'd like your perspective on my stakeholder management during the Q3 project. Specifically, how effectively did I keep you informed?"
- "I'd appreciate your view on my leadership during the recent crisis. What did I do well, and what could I improve?"
- "Help me understand how I'm doing on technical depth. Are there areas where I need to strengthen my skills?"
